Participate

Three ways to participate

Archival papers

Long and short papers (see page limits in Call for papers) for publication in the ACL Anthology, including technical and position papers where appropriate, subject to acceptance and camera-ready requirements.

Non-archival papers

Extended abstract track. Short unpublished write-ups (see page limits in Call for papers) for feedback and discussion without archival publication.

Previously published or accepted work. Share relevant results from other venues in a non-archival presentation; details in Call for papers.

Shared-task system reports

Papers describing systems, analyses, and error studies on the pluralistic value benchmarks; may overlap with teams registering for the shared task leaderboard.

The program is planned as a one-day workshop with oral presentations, posters, a panel, invited talks, and a dedicated shared-task session. We anticipate roughly 30–50 paper submissions plus 10–20 shared-task team reports (non-binding estimates from the workshop proposal).
