Welcome to the First Workshop on Pluralistic Value Alignment of LLMs (PlurVA-LLM)!

Welcome to the First Workshop on Pluralistic Value Alignment of LLMs (PlurVA-LLM). Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly shaping how people access information, make decisions, and interact with AI in everyday life. As these systems become more powerful and widely deployed, ensuring that they align with human values has become an important challenge. However, most existing research on LLM alignment has focused either on broad safety objectives or on alignment to a single value framework. Such approaches may not fully capture the diversity, contextuality, and potential conflicts that characterize real-world human values.

PlurVA-LLM focuses on the challenge of pluralistic value alignment: how to build LLMs that can recognize, reason about, and respond to diverse values across individuals, communities, and contexts. The workshop aims to provide a venue for discussing the conceptual foundations, technical methods, and evaluation approaches needed to better understand this problem. By bringing together researchers from NLP, AI alignment, social science, philosophy, and related fields, PlurVA-LLM seeks to encourage interdisciplinary exchange on the role of pluralistic values in the development and deployment of LLMs.

To facilitate this exchange of ideas, we will feature invited talks and panel discussions with leading researchers, alongside long and short paper submissions. All accepted papers will be presented in an oral session (hybrid).

Call for papers

Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly deployed in real-world applications, raising urgent concerns about the values they encode, reflect, and prioritize. While value alignment has become a central topic in AI research, existing studies have largely focused on generic safety or alignment to a single core value (i.e., value monism), leaving the challenge of pluralistic value alignment underexplored. In practice, human values are diverse and context-dependent. Building LLMs that can recognize, reason about, and align with pluralistic values is therefore both a technical and societal challenge.

The First Workshop on Pluralistic Value Alignment of LLMs (PlurVA-LLM) aims to provide a dedicated venue for advancing research on this emerging topic. The workshop will bring together researchers from NLP, machine learning, AI safety, social science, philosophy, and related fields to discuss the foundations, methods, evaluation, and applications of pluralistic value alignment in LLMs.

Topics of interest

We welcome submissions on topics including, but not limited to:

  • Theoretical foundations and formalizations of pluralistic value alignment
  • Alignment methods for pluralistic values in LLMs
  • Benchmarks and evaluation protocols for pluralistic value alignment
  • Human-AI collaboration for constructing and curating value-sensitive datasets
  • Interpretability and analysis of value alignment in LLMs
  • Pluralistic value alignment in downstream applications and real-world deployment
  • Multilingual, multicultural, and low-resource perspectives on value alignment
  • Pluralistic value alignment in multimodal models and systems

The workshop will also host a shared task on evaluating LLMs' value alignment and normative reasoning across adversarial, daily, and principle-driven settings.

Submission tracks

We invite submissions to both archival and non-archival tracks.

Archival track

Long papers (up to 8 pages) and short papers (up to 4 pages), with unlimited pages for references. Submissions can be new papers (double-anonymous review) or papers previously reviewed through ARR.

Accepted archival papers may use one additional page in camera-ready and will be published in the ACL Anthology. Previously published work is not eligible for this track.

Non-archival · Extended abstract

2-4 pages plus up to 2 pages of references. Suitable for position papers and early-stage work that benefits from peer feedback. Uses the same double-anonymous review process.

Non-archival · Previously published/accepted

For work already reviewed, published, or accepted elsewhere. Include venue/journal information and archived link if available. This track is reviewed for relevance to workshop themes and has no page limit.

Accepted papers in both non-archival tracks are invited for workshop presentation, but will not be published in the ACL Anthology.

Dual submission is permitted: papers that are under review at other venues may be submitted, provided that this is disclosed at the time of submission. If a paper is accepted both here and elsewhere, authors may choose to move it to the non-archival track upon acceptance.

Benchmark suite & shared task

The shared task evaluates LLMs’ value alignment and normative reasoning across adversarial, daily, and principle-driven settings. It is organized around three benchmarks in Indonesian, Sri Lankan (English + Sinhala), and Chinese (the Chinese track may use attack-style or scenario-style setups comparable to the other two). The competition platform, full timeline, and registration will be announced later.

  • Evaluation protocol — a dedicated scoring framework for the task.
  • Baselines — reference implementations or scores for comparison.
  • Evaluation code — release policy will be announced.

Dataset statistics below come from benchmark papers and proposals; final rules may differ from what is on this page.

D2VBench

Chinese · scenario-class everyday dilemmas

Built on a four-layer hierarchical value system (Livelihood, Social Order, Civilization) with 158 fine-grained leaf concepts and on the order of ~10k instances. Scenarios are constructed through a four-stage pipeline (concept inventory → generation → quality control → reference & judge calibration), with Align-Score, five-dimensional rationale quality, a structured reason bank, and alignment metrics against human and referee models—suited for normative reasoning beyond single-label classification. A system-level proposal also targets on the order of ~20k scenario samples with free-form responses vs. human-curated references.

Indonesian · Pancasila-Dilemmas

Indonesia · multiple-choice value dilemmas

1,834 four-option items grounded in the Pancasila five principles, sourced from Kompas headlines with crowd and expert validation. Evaluation can report accuracy and distributional agreement (e.g. JSD / TVD), including majoritarian vs. “any-agreement” style metrics against human majorities or full distributions.

LKvaluesBench (Sri Lanka)

Sri Lanka · bilingual judgment alignment

2,000 instances in English and Sinhala (paired translations) over 40 survey-derived societal values. Labels indicate which language carries the judgment (A / B / BOTH / neither). Reports model–human alignment and cross-lingual consistency—testing whether “alignment” transfers across languages within one cultural context.

Detailed task description, data specifications, baselines, and leaderboards will be released with the official shared-task announcement. Participation is expected from on the order of 10–20 teams (proposal estimate), with results presented in a dedicated session.

Three ways to participate

Archival papers

Long and short papers (see page limits in Call for papers) for publication in the ACL Anthology, including technical and position papers where appropriate, subject to acceptance and camera-ready requirements.

Non-archival papers

For interdisciplinary participation, work in neighboring fields, or results already published or under review elsewhere where allowed—supporting dialogue without duplicating archival constraints.

Shared-task system reports

Papers describing systems, analyses, and error studies on the pluralistic value benchmarks; may overlap with teams registering for the shared task leaderboard.

  • Diverse organizing committee composition.
  • Support for students and scholars with limited travel budgets (subject to sponsorship).
  • Hybrid participation so remote attendees can follow key sessions.
